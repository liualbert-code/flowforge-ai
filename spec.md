# FlowForge AI: The Creative Workflow Foundry

## 1. Manifesto: The Core Belief
In an era of AI-generated noise, FlowForge AI believes that **true value lies in the friction between ideas.** We reject "seamless" automation that leads to generic outputs. Our mission is to forge high-impact, soul-infused creative workflows by intentionally designing systems that challenge assumptions, embrace constructive conflict, and reserve space for human intuition.

## 2. Company Operating Philosophy
- **Friction-Driven Excellence:** We don't avoid disagreement between agents; we mandate it.
- **Human-at-the-Helm:** AI handles the heavy lifting of research and drafting, but humans provide the "North Star" and the final touch of soul.
- **Actionable Precision:** Every output must be implementable "by next Monday."
- **Anti-Cliché Policy:** We strictly avoid generic AI buzzwords; our language is sharp, specific, and grounded.

## 3. Service Catalog
- **The Workflow Audit:** Diagnosing bottlenecks in existing creative processes.
- **The Agentic Blueprint:** Designing custom multi-agent systems for specific business needs.
- **The "Soul-Infusion" Workshop:** Training teams to integrate AI without losing their unique brand voice.

## 4. The 3 Specialists (AI Agents)
- **The Friction Architect (Agent A):** 
    - *Role:* Identifies where a process is "too easy" or likely to produce generic results.
    - *Bottom Line:* Demands more constraints and challenges to the status quo.
- **The Efficiency Scythe (Agent B):** 
    - *Role:* Focuses on radical simplification and removal of redundant steps.
    - *Bottom Line:* If a step doesn't directly contribute to the final value, it must be cut.
- **The Cultural Anthropologist (Agent C):** 
    - *Role:* Ensures the workflow respects the human context and brand heritage.
    - *Bottom Line:* Protects the "soul" of the project from being automated away.

## 5. COO Workflow (The Orchestrator)
1. **Intake (`/onboard`):** Analyzes the client brief for "Heart" (intent) and "Hurdles" (constraints).
2. **Dispatch (`/forge`):** Assigns the brief to the 3 Specialists.
3. **The Conflict Stage:** Specialists present their drafts. The COO identifies "Tension Points" where goals overlap or contradict.
4. **Arbitration Meeting:**
    - Max 3 rounds of structured debate.
    - COO mediates to find a "High-Value Synthesis."
    - Final COO ruling if no consensus is reached.
5. **Human Integration:** The founder adds the "X-Factor"—intuition and field experience that AI cannot replicate.
6. **Delivery (`/deliver`):** Final package assembly and workspace archiving.

## 6. Deliverable Structure
- **Chapter 1: The Diagnosis:** What is currently broken?
- **Chapter 2: The Forge Map:** The visual and logical flow of the new system.
- **Chapter 3: The Toolset:** Specific MCPs, prompts, and software required.
- **Chapter 4: The Conflict Report:** Insights gained from the agent debate phase.
- **Chapter 5: The Implementation Guide:** Step-by-step actions for next week.
- **Chapter 6: The Founder’s Intuition:** The human "soul" of the project.

## 7. Agent Collaboration Structure
- **Shared Memory:** A local file-based system (`./vault`) where all session context and past learnings are stored.
- **The Tension Log:** A mandatory document tracking every disagreement and its resolution.
- **Self-QC Protocol:** Each agent must score their own work against "Actionability" and "Uniqueness" before submission.
